Storytelling with Data

Devices and Usage

As a data science undergrad, I have always been fascinated by information and numbers. Recently, as I was planning to buy a new device, I conducted a survey among students at my college about the devices they use. I gained some fascinating insights, which I present in this blog.

Data Collection

For the data collection ,I floated a google survey form among the students of the college which consisted of the following questions:-

  • User's age
  • Brand of Smartphone
  • How long they have been using the device?
  • Price of smartphone

I received a very positive response and a total of 125 entries were recieved,Now after this the data was formulated into a xlsx from which we recieved a .csv file upon which the further operations were performed.

Data Cleaning and Pre Processing

The following operations were performed:-

  • Converted Data Types: The ‘Price of Smartphone’ column was converted to a numeric type for proper analysis.
  • Handled Missing Values: Missing values in the ‘Price of Smartphone’ column were replaced with the median price.
  • Removed Anomalies:
    • Entries with unrealistic smartphone ages (>10 years) were removed.
    • Extreme price values (>₹100,000) were also excluded.

Summary Statistics

After cleaning and augmentation, the dataset contains 80 entrieswere left remaining and their Key statistics include:

  • Average smartphone age: 2.1 years
  • Median smartphone price: ₹20,000
  • Price range: ₹10,000 – ₹70,000
  • Age range of respondents: 17 – 28 years

Visualizations and Insights

  • Age Distribution

    The majority of respondents are between 18-21 years old, indicating a younger demographic which is becuase majority of studetns in the insitute are pursuing bachelors.

  • Smartphone Age Distribution

    Most users have smartphones aged between 1-3 years, suggesting frequent upgrades.

  • Price Distribution

    The price distribution is skewed, with most smartphones priced between ₹15,000 - ₹25,000. A few premium smartphones (₹70,000+) are owned by a small fraction of users.
